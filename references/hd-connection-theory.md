# Human Design — Connection Theory

> How two bodygraphs read as a relationship. Where synastry reads the planets
> between two charts, connection theory reads the **channels** between two
> designs: which circuits each person wires, and what happens when you put the
> two gate sets side by side. This is rich and underused in consumer products —
> lead on it when the data is present.
>
> Computed through the `SYNASTRY_CONTRIBUTORS` registry. Receipts (specific
> channels) live in the relational Deep Dive; the Blueprint reports the verdict
> jargon-free. No score.

---

## The data

Each person's bodygraph (from the HD API, second call for person B) yields a set
of **defined gates**. A **channel** is a pair of gates (there are 36). The
connection between two people, channel by channel, is exactly one of four types
— determined purely by who holds which gates.

The HD layer only runs when **both** birth times are known and the HD service is
available. Without it, the reading falls back to the other systems and says so
rather than inventing a design.

---

## The four connection types

For each of the 36 channels `[g1, g2]`, with A and B's gate sets:

- **Electromagnetic** — each person holds one half, and together they complete
  the channel (neither has it alone). The classic attraction: a magnetic pull
  with a built-in love/irritation flip on exactly those wires. This is the
  signature "opposites attract" mechanic.
- **Companionship** — both people fully wire the same channel. Easy, reinforcing
  sameness; you instinctively understand each other here. Risk: an echo chamber,
  no productive difference.
- **Dominance** — one person fully wires the channel; the other holds neither
  gate. The wired person consistently sets the tone there and conditions the
  open person. Not bad, but one-directional — name who leads where.
- **Compromise** — one person fully wires the channel; the other holds just one
  of the two gates. The half-wired person's single gate gets consistently
  activated and somewhat overpowered. A reliable spot that needs ongoing
  negotiation.

A channel where the two sets don't form any of these (e.g. a single shared
hanging gate, or no overlap) is simply not a connection and is ignored.

## How it feeds the reading

- **Convergence first.** When the HD connection echoes the synastry — e.g.
  electromagnetic channels *and* Sun–Moon/Venus–Mars contacts both saying
  *attraction* — that agreement, stated once, is the Blueprint line. Cross-
  tradition agreement (HD + Western + BaZi) is the strongest signal available.
- **Lead on electromagnetics** for romantic readings: the half-each-completes
  mechanic is the most legible, most "how did it know that" piece in the system.
- **Dominance and compromise** are where friction and negotiation live — feed
  the "where it snags" and "how to move through it" beats, named directionally
  (who leads, who negotiates), never as a verdict on the relationship.

## Discipline (unchanged)

No score, no percentage, no "should you." Connection theory describes the wiring
between two people and how to move through it — weather, not a verdict. Channels
are receipts: name them in the Deep Dive, not in the Blueprint.
