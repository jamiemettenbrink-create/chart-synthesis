# Human Design: Types, Authorities, and Profiles

## TYPES

### Generator
- **Energy:** Sustainable, renewable sacral life force. The builders and sustainers.
- **Strategy:** Respond — wait for something in the environment to activate the gut response before acting
- **Sign:** Satisfaction (aligned) / Frustration (not-self)
- **Key insight:** Generators are not meant to initiate. Initiating burns them out and creates wrong-fit situations. The gut response (an involuntary sound or physical sensation of "uh-huh" vs "uh-uh") is the correct signal, not the mind's reasoning.
- **Practical:** Ask yes/no questions that can be answered with the gut. "Does this feel expansive or contracting?" Not "what do I think about this?"

### Manifesting Generator
- Same sacral energy as Generator, but with a direct manifestation channel
- Strategy: Respond THEN inform before acting
- Designed for multi-passionate, non-linear paths — skipping steps is often correct

### Manifestor
- Initiating energy — the only type truly designed to start things without waiting
- Strategy: Inform (not ask permission — just inform those affected before acting)
- Sign: Peace (aligned) / Anger (not-self)
- ~8% of population; often feel resistance from the world to their initiating nature

### Projector
- Focused, penetrating awareness — designed to guide and direct others' energy
- Strategy: Wait for the invitation (especially for big life decisions: career, love, home)
- Sign: Success (aligned) / Bitterness (not-self)
- Need recognition before sharing wisdom; giving unsolicited advice depletes them

### Reflector
- Open, sampling, mirroring — reflects the health of their community
- Strategy: Wait a full lunar cycle before major decisions
- Sign: Surprise (aligned) / Disappointment (not-self)
- Extremely rare (~1%); environment and community are everything

## AUTHORITIES

### Emotional Authority (Solar Plexus defined)
**Most important:** There is NO truth in the now. Clarity only comes after the emotional wave has completed a cycle.

- The wave moves from hope/excitement → pain/disappointment → back up
- Decisions made at peak excitement OR peak low are both distorted
- The practice: Sleep on it. For major decisions, wait days or weeks.
- Clarity signal: When something feels "okay" across multiple emotional states — not neutral-flat, but consistently okay.
- The phrase: "I need time to feel into this" — not avoidance, correct process
- **What trips emotional authority people up:** Deciding to relieve the discomfort of uncertainty. The discomfort IS the wave — it's not a sign the answer is no.
- Works with: All types. Most common authority.

### Sacral Authority (Sacral defined, Solar Plexus undefined)
- Gut response is immediate and reliable
- The gut sound: "uh-huh" (yes) / "uh-uh" (no) — often involuntary
- Best accessed through yes/no questions in the moment
- Unlike Emotional Authority, clarity IS available now

### Splenic Authority (Spleen defined, others undefined)
- Speaks once, quietly, in the moment — and never repeats itself
- Instinctual survival intelligence; the body knows
- The challenge: The voice is subtle and easy to override with mind or emotion
- Practice: Learn to hear the first quiet whisper before the mind drowns it out

### Self-Projected Authority (G Center defined, Throat connected)
- Truth comes through hearing yourself talk
- Needs a trusted "sounding board" — not for advice, just to speak out loud and hear their own truth emerge
- Does NOT take the listener's advice; the value is in the speaking, not the response received

### Ego/Heart Authority
- Rare; decisions come from what the heart/will genuinely wants, not obligation
- "What do I want?" is a legitimate and complete decision-making framework
- Not selfish — correct for this type

### Mental/No Inner Authority (Projectors only)
- No reliable inner signal; truth emerges through dialogue with trusted others over time
- The outer environment is the authority — right people, right place, right conversations

### Lunar Authority (Reflectors only)
- Full 28-day lunar cycle before major decisions
- Experience all 64 gates as the moon transits; different perspectives each day
- Journaling through the cycle reveals the truth

## PROFILES

### 1/3 — Investigator/Martyr
- Needs a solid foundation of knowledge before feeling secure
- Learns through trial and error; "failures" are data, not verdicts
- The 3rd line biography always looks messier than others' — this is correct

### 2/4 — Hermit/Opportunist
- Natural talent that doesn't always see itself clearly
- Needs alone time to recharge (hermit) but thrives through network (opportunist)
- Called out of hermitage by others who recognize their gifts

### 3/5 — Martyr/Heretic *(Jamie's profile)*
- **Line 3:** The Great Experimenter. Life is a laboratory. Learns by discovering what doesn't work. The biography is full of trial-and-error — relationships, careers, beliefs. This is not failure; it is the design's data-gathering method. The wound: shame around the "mistakes."
- **Line 5:** The Practical Visionary / Heretic. Carries a powerful projection field — others project both savior idealization AND shadow disappointment onto them. They are seen as MORE capable than they are, then criticized for being human. The work: don't take on either projection as identity.
- **Together:** Someone who learns through experience AND is projected onto by others. The integration: use the experimental wisdom as genuine practical guidance for others, without needing to live up to others' inflated projections.

### 4/6 — Opportunist/Role Model
- First 30 years: experimenting (like a 3/6 in Line 3 phase)
- After Saturn Return (~30): moves onto the "roof" — observing, integrating
- After ~50: comes down as an embodied role model; their life IS the teaching

### 5/1 — Heretic/Investigator
- The universal projection field at its most intense
- Called to solve problems for the collective — but at risk of being blamed when solutions don't work perfectly
- Needs solid 1st line foundation to back up the 5th line pressure to deliver

### 6/2 — Role Model/Hermit
- Three-phase life similar to 4/6
- Natural role model who doesn't always see their own exemplary quality
- The hermit needs genuine alone time — it's not antisocial, it's correct
