# Enneagram: Types, Wings, Instinctual Variants & Chart Integration

## How the Enneagram Fits This System

The Enneagram cannot be calculated — it is self-identified or assessed. It is included as
an optional enrichment field in the input form. When provided, it deepens the
Shadow Patterns section considerably because the Enneagram is the most precise system
available for naming the specific flavor of unconscious reactivity.

**Key principle:** The Enneagram describes the ego structure's defense mechanism —
the strategy the personality adopted early in life to feel safe. It is not a
personality type in the positive sense (astrology covers that better) but a
map of the specific contraction, fear, and compulsion.

**Integration in synthesis:** Always connect the Enneagram type to the chart's
shadow signatures. A Type 2 with Moon in Scorpio reads differently than a Type 2
with Moon in Gemini. The Enneagram names the wound; the chart shows where it lives.

## THE NINE TYPES

### Type 1 — The Reformer / Perfectionist
- **Core Fear:** Being corrupt, defective, or wrong
- **Core Desire:** To be good, to have integrity, to be right
- **Basic Compulsion:** Improving, reforming, criticizing
- **Unconscious Message Received:** "It's not okay to make mistakes"
- **Defense Mechanism:** Reaction Formation (turning forbidden impulses into their opposite)
- **Shadow:** Resentment — the chronic, low-grade anger at a world that falls short of standards
- **Gift:** Discernment — a genuine moral compass and the ability to see clearly what is broken
- **At Best:** Wise, discerning, realistic, noble, ethical
- **Under Stress (→ 4):** Becomes melancholic, withdrawn, self-pitying
- **In Growth (→ 7):** Becomes spontaneous, joyful, embracing imperfection
- **Instinct subtypes:**
  - SP: Anxiety about adequacy; perfectionism about their own conduct; the "worried" One
  - SO: Advocacy; social reform; the "crusader" One; most outwardly critical
  - SX: Anger expressed more directly; perfectionism focused on the partner; "jealous" One

### Type 2 — The Helper / Giver
- **Core Fear:** Being unwanted, unloved, or without value to others
- **Core Desire:** To be loved, to be needed, to feel indispensable
- **Basic Compulsion:** Helping, pleasing, anticipating others' needs
- **Unconscious Message Received:** "It's not okay to have your own needs"
- **Defense Mechanism:** Repression (pushing own needs into unconscious)
- **Shadow:** Pride — the unconscious inflation that says "I give and they can't survive without me"
- **Gift:** Care — genuine, attuned nurturance; the ability to sense what another person actually needs
- **At Best:** Empathetic, generous, warm, self-nurturing, loving without strings
- **Under Stress (→ 8):** Becomes controlling, aggressive, demanding
- **In Growth (→ 4):** Becomes self-aware, emotionally honest, knows own needs
- **Instinct subtypes:**
  - SP: Privileges and exemptions; the helpfulness is more self-focused; "privileged" Two
  - SO: Ambition through service; helping the powerful; the "ambitious" Two
  - SX: Seduction and merging; focused helpfulness on one person; "seductive" Two
- **Chart correlation:** South Node Libra, Moon in Cancer, Gate 27 shadow

### Type 3 — The Achiever / Performer
- **Core Fear:** Being worthless, failing, being exposed as a fraud
- **Core Desire:** To feel valuable, to be successful, to be admired
- **Basic Compulsion:** Achieving, performing, image management
- **Unconscious Message Received:** "It's not okay to have your own feelings and identity"
- **Defense Mechanism:** Identification (becoming the role/persona)
- **Shadow:** Deceit — the unconscious self-deception about who one actually is beneath the performance
- **Gift:** Efficiency and Inspiration — genuine capacity to achieve and model what's possible for others
- **At Best:** Self-accepting, authentic, modest, admirable, inspiring
- **Under Stress (→ 9):** Becomes checked out, disengaged, apathetic
- **In Growth (→ 6):** Becomes loyal, committed, able to show vulnerability
- **Instinct subtypes:**
  - SP: Security through material success; the "good person" Three who downplays achievement
  - SO: The archetypal Three; success and status as social currency; prestige-driven
  - SX: Charisma and attraction; masculine/feminine ideals; the "masculine/feminine" Three

### Type 4 — The Individualist / Romantic
- **Core Fear:** Having no identity, being ordinary, being without personal significance
- **Core Desire:** To be unique, to find the self, to have personal significance
- **Basic Compulsion:** Cultivating uniqueness, withdrawing, longing
- **Unconscious Message Received:** "It's not okay to be too comfortable or happy"
- **Defense Mechanism:** Introjection (taking in and identifying with external objects)
- **Shadow:** Envy — the chronic comparison between one's own perceived deficiency and others' wholeness
- **Gift:** Depth and Creativity — genuine emotional intelligence; the capacity to transform suffering into beauty
- **At Best:** Creative, expressive, self-aware, emotionally honest, inspired
- **Under Stress (→ 2):** Becomes clingy, manipulative, focused on pleasing
- **In Growth (→ 1):** Becomes principled, objective, action-oriented
- **Instinct subtypes:**
  - SP: Stoic suffering; toughness as identity; "reckless" or "daring" Four; endurance-based
  - SO: Shame and comparison with the social group; the most classically "romantic" Four
  - SX: Competition and envy focused on the beloved; intensity; the "infatuation" Four
- **Chart correlation:** Scorpio Moon, Pluto in 7th, 12th house stellium

### Type 5 — The Investigator / Observer
- **Core Fear:** Being incapable, overwhelmed, helpless, or depleted by others' demands
- **Core Desire:** To be capable and competent, to understand, to have enough resources
- **Basic Compulsion:** Gathering knowledge, withdrawing, conserving energy
- **Unconscious Message Received:** "It's not okay to be comfortable in the world"
- **Defense Mechanism:** Isolation (separating feelings from thoughts)
- **Shadow:** Avarice — the hoarding of knowledge, time, and energy; the withdrawal that prevents life
- **Gift:** Insight — genuine depth of understanding; the capacity to see what others miss by going all the way in
- **At Best:** Open-minded, perceptive, pioneering, innovative, absorbed in wonder
- **Under Stress (→ 7):** Becomes scattered, impulsive, hyperactive
- **In Growth (→ 8):** Becomes confident, decisive, taking action in the world
- **Chart correlation:** Mercury in 12th house, Saturn-Uranus in 8th, Virgo emphasis

### Type 6 — The Loyalist / Skeptic
- **Core Fear:** Being without support, guidance, or security; being abandoned
- **Core Desire:** To have security, support, and guidance; to trust
- **Basic Compulsion:** Testing, questioning, seeking reassurance, building alliances
- **Unconscious Message Received:** "It's not okay to trust yourself"
- **Defense Mechanism:** Projection (attributing own fears to the environment)
- **Shadow:** Fear — the chronic scanning for threat; doubt about one's own perception
- **Gift:** Loyalty and Courage — once committed, extraordinary faithfulness; facing fear directly
- **At Best:** Courageous, reliable, trustworthy, responsible, funny under pressure
- **Under Stress (→ 3):** Becomes image-conscious, competitive, workaholic
- **In Growth (→ 9):** Becomes relaxed, trusting, peaceful, open to others' goodwill
- **Subtypes:**
  - Phobic Six: seeks allies and authority figures; freezes or appease under threat
  - Counter-phobic Six: charges toward fear; appears aggressive or daring; can look like 8

### Type 7 — The Enthusiast / Epicure
- **Core Fear:** Being deprived, trapped in pain, missing out
- **Core Desire:** To be satisfied, to have enough, to be happy and fulfilled
- **Basic Compulsion:** Planning pleasures, reframing negatives, staying in motion
- **Unconscious Message Received:** "It's not okay to depend on anyone for anything"
- **Defense Mechanism:** Rationalization (reframing limiting experiences as positive)
- **Shadow:** Gluttony — the compulsive need for more; the inability to be nourished by what is
- **Gift:** Enthusiasm and Synthesis — genuine joy in ideas; the ability to see connections across fields
- **At Best:** Joyful, satisfied, grateful, focused, realistic about limitations
- **Under Stress (→ 1):** Becomes critical, perfectionistic, rigid
- **In Growth (→ 5):** Becomes focused, deep, able to stay with difficulty
- **Chart correlation:** Jupiter in prominent position, Sagittarius emphasis, Gate 35

### Type 8 — The Challenger / Protector
- **Core Fear:** Being controlled, harmed, violated, or at the mercy of others
- **Core Desire:** To protect themselves and those they care for; to be autonomous
- **Basic Compulsion:** Asserting, controlling, pushing, testing limits
- **Unconscious Message Received:** "It's not okay to be vulnerable or trust others"
- **Defense Mechanism:** Denial (of vulnerability and weakness)
- **Shadow:** Lust — the excess of intensity; the hunger for more contact with life that overrides others
- **Gift:** Strength and Protection — genuine power in service of the vulnerable; magnanimous leadership
- **At Best:** Self-restrained, merciful, magnanimous, heroic, a force for good
- **Under Stress (→ 5):** Becomes secretive, withdrawn, isolated
- **In Growth (→ 2):** Becomes open-hearted, nurturing, generous
- **Chart correlation:** Mars conjunct MC, Scorpio Ascendant, Gate 34, Pluto angular

### Type 9 — The Peacemaker / Mediator
- **Core Fear:** Loss of connection, conflict, fragmentation
- **Core Desire:** Inner stability, peace, union
- **Basic Compulsion:** Merging with others' agendas, avoiding conflict, numbing
- **Unconscious Message Received:** "It's not okay to assert yourself; your presence doesn't matter"
- **Defense Mechanism:** Narcotization (numbing through routine, comfort, or distraction)
- **Shadow:** Sloth — not physical laziness but spiritual inertia; forgetting the self's importance
- **Gift:** Harmony and Receptivity — the capacity to see all sides; a natural mediator; genuine serenity
- **At Best:** Indomitable, all-embracing, alive, self-possessed, natural
- **Under Stress (→ 6):** Becomes anxious, seeking reassurance, worried about security
- **In Growth (→ 3):** Becomes self-developing, energetic, goal-directed
- **Chart correlation:** Moon in 7th house, Libra stellium, Gate 9 shadow

## WINGS

Each type has access to one of its two adjacent types as a "wing" — a secondary flavor that modifies the core type.

- **1w9:** More withdrawn, idealistic, removed
- **1w2:** More interpersonally engaged, helpful, warmer
- **2w1:** More principled and mission-driven
- **2w3:** More image-conscious, ambitious, driven
- **3w2:** More people-pleasing, charming, relational
- **3w4:** More introspective, image-focused, melancholic
- **4w3:** More extroverted, ambitious, image-aware
- **4w5:** More withdrawn, intellectual, cerebral
- **5w4:** More creative, artistic, subjective
- **5w6:** More loyal, anxious, practical, team-oriented
- **6w5:** More introverted, analytical, self-reliant
- **6w7:** More playful, optimistic, outgoing
- **7w6:** More loyal, anxious, relationship-focused
- **7w8:** More assertive, materialistic, impatient
- **8w7:** More extroverted, pleasure-seeking, impulsive
- **8w9:** More contained, diplomatic, gentle giant
- **9w8:** More assertive, direct, stubborn
- **9w1:** More principled, ordered, idealistic

## INSTINCTUAL VARIANTS (Subtypes)

Three instinctual drives that modify how each type expresses its core pattern:

**Self-Preservation (SP):** Primary focus on physical safety, health, comfort, and material security. The most grounded and practical subtype. Anxiety is most physical.

**Social (SO):** Primary focus on position within groups, social roles, belonging, and prestige. Most aware of social dynamics. Anxiety is about belonging.

**Sexual / One-to-One (SX):** Primary focus on intense connection, intimacy, and attraction. Seeks fusion with an ideal. Anxiety is about the quality of primary relationship.

**The Stacking:** Everyone has all three but in a hierarchy. The lead instinct is most dominant; the repressed instinct (last in stack) is often where the shadow is most hidden.

## INTEGRATION IN SYNTHESIS

When an Enneagram type is provided, incorporate it into:

**Shadow Patterns section:** Name the specific shadow mechanism
- "The [Type X] structure means the specific contraction here is [shadow]. This shows up in this chart as [house/placement connection]."

**Decision-Making section:** Each type has a characteristic wrong-making-process
- Type 2: Decides based on what will be most needed/loved; misses own truth
- Type 5: Decides after exhaustive information gathering; misses the moment
- Type 7: Decides based on what sounds most exciting; misses depth

**Relationship section:** Each type has a relational dynamic
- Type 4: Pushes and pulls; idealizes then criticizes
- Type 6: Tests loyalty; fear-based attachment

**Growth edge:** The integration arrow points to the direction of health
- "Moving toward [integration type] looks like..."

**Cross-system note:** When Enneagram shadow matches Gene Keys shadow or South Node pattern, name the convergence explicitly — it's the most powerful signal in the reading.
